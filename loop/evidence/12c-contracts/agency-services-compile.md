# 12c micro-dogfood · agency-services compile

FIXTURE. The firm below is invented for compiler testing only. This file contains proof REQUIREMENTS, never proof content; no client, case, or number appears here or may be reused anywhere. The point of this compile is to exercise the new question-1 mapping and the contract's boundaries against `saas-homepage` (enterprise variant) and `personal-home`, the two archetypes that previously absorbed services briefs badly.

Fixture brief, in full: a three-partner application-security consultancy selling review and remediation engagements to engineering leaders at mid-size product companies. Engagements have a stated minimum. Traffic: mostly referrals from past clients, plus some search for the service category. The partners do the work themselves. This property has no `foundry-log.md` yet.

## The five questions

1. **Conversion:** a booked qualified call. → `agency-services`, per the compiler's new question-1 mapping. Not `saas-homepage`'s enterprise variant: there is no product and no self-serve path; the firm sells engagements, and the page is measured on qualified calls, not demos. Not `personal-home`: the seller is a firm with a roster and a process, not a person picking one primary action.
2. **Relationship:** ongoing within an engagement, episodic across them; the process job carries the weight a product page would give a how-it-works.
3. **Traffic:** mixed (referral plus category search); no single source, so message match binds only loosely and navigation is standard.
4. **Awareness state:** referral arrivals are product-aware of the firm and checking fit; search arrivals are solution-aware, comparing firms. Referral dominates per the brief, and the compile records that.
5. **Decision weight:** heavy; a minimum-sized engagement is a serious budget line, and a committee approves it.

## Filled contract

**Goal.** A booked qualified call with an engineering leader whose problem and budget clear the firm's stated minimum.

**Buyer entry states.** Both contract states arrive, referral-dominant per the brief. The committee note is KEPT: the visitor sells the engagement internally, so the case-study material must work as a sendable artifact.

**Jobs, kept or struck.**

- Positioning line naming the client and the result: KEPT.
- Case studies as walked decisions: KEPT; the fixture supplies none, per the fabrication rule, so the compile records the requirement and the run would stop to collect real ones.
- Process from first call to delivered work: KEPT; the partners-do-the-work fact is the staffing answer and goes in this section.
- Fit line: KEPT; the stated engagement minimum is its anchor.
- Pricing posture: KEPT; the minimum can be said, so it is said.
- People: KEPT; three partners, no delegated team to disclose.
- Qualifying booking form: KEPT; fields limited to what the first call needs, each with a stated qualifying purpose in the spec.

**Axes.**

- Narrative shape: proof-led; referral traffic arrives pre-sold on credibility and reads the work first.
- Hero form: copy-plus-CTA; there is no product to put in the hero, and the firm is not one person.
- Proof strategy: concentrated; a walked case study is a heavy artifact, and the fixture's inventory is few-and-heavy, not many-and-small.
- Density: mid; the decision is heavy but referral warmth carries much of the persuasion, so the page qualifies rather than convinces at length. The spec records this as the compile's judgment call.

**Ordering constraints engaged.** The shared ten, plus the contract's three: fit line and pricing posture before the form, process before pricing posture, people before the final ask. The form-budget inversion is recorded in the spec with each field's purpose, per the CTA policy.

**Boundary check.** A solo consultant with the same offer compiles `personal-home` with book-a-call primary, not this contract; the firm-ness (roster, process, staffing disclosure) is what selects `agency-services`. A firm launching a productized subscription service would straddle toward `saas-homepage` and merge per the compiler.
