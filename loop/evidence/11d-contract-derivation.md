## Two-buyer derivation (structural variation check)

### newsletter-capture

- Buyer A, content-led: one post went wide, single-source traffic. Legal order: hero (exchange + form + taste-it link as the hero proof element, message-matched to the post) → what is inside → expectations (`5 minutes, weekly`) → writer → close form with privacy line. Constraints 1, 2, 7, 8 satisfied.
- Buyer B, personality-led: podcast followers who come for the person. Legal order: hero (exchange framed around the writer, form + creditable subscriber count) → writer (the hook, directly after the hero) → best-issue links → what is inside + expectations → close form. Structurally different from A (writer second vs fourth, proof shape count vs taste-link); both satisfy the full constraint set.
- Illegal order, rejected: form-only hero with no proof element in the hero viewport, proof deferred to mid-page. Violates ordering constraint 1 and shared constraints 2 and 7 (mobile reader hits the form before any proof).

### personal-home

- Buyer A, name-searcher doing diligence, primary action book-a-call. Legal order: hero (positioning + CTA, proof-of-work grid within the viewport) → proof of work → current focus (qualification) → credibility narrative → routes → contact. Proof-led opening.
- Buyer B, referred from a talk, primary action read-the-work. Legal order: hero connecting to the talk (shared constraint 8) → credibility narrative (completing the picture the talk started) → proof of work → current focus → routes + contact. Narrative-led opening; structurally different from A, fully legal.
- Illegal order, rejected: hero → routes → contact → narrative → proof of work last. The hero CTA and the routes block both sit with no proof element within one viewport (shared constraint 2), and the mobile reader reaches the contact form before any proof (shared constraint 7).
